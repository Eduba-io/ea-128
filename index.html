01 / The Eduba frame

Most infrastructure-first companies have an AI layer they have not built yet.

We work on a simple idea. About 60% of what a federal-vendor team ships is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built, and documenting the sort so a customer review can follow it cleanly.

Applied to the Fognigma stack, the map resolves quickly. Multi-cloud orchestration, encryption, and routing are code-and-infrastructure work. Tradecraft constraints on deployment, customer-specific posture, and access control are rule-based logic. The genuinely AI surface, if a decision is made to build one, lives above the MPN telemetry: anomaly detection, analyst-assist summarization, operator-friendly pattern matching. That is a small, bounded surface that earns its keep quickly without pulling the infrastructure team off core work.

That is the work we would propose to discuss with the Dexter Edward team, at whatever pace the customer base allows.

03 / The underlying research

The methodology is published.

Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture, submitted to ACM TiiS. The core claim is that agent and documentation context can be organized as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. Useful whenever a small team is responsible for documentation that has to survive a customer review. Open source under MIT license.
